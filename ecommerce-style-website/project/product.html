About this resource

The 12-Week Recovery Journey is a structured session-by-session facilitation guide designed for peer recovery specialists running ongoing support groups. Each week builds on the last — moving through group formation, self-reflection, skills development, and integration. The format is clear and consistent so both first-time and experienced facilitators feel grounded from session one.

What's inside

  • 12 complete session plans with facilitator scripts and timing
  • Opening and closing ritual suggestions for each session
  • Check-in and check-out prompts for group climate tracking
  • Printable participant worksheets for reflection exercises
  • Adaptation notes for drop-in vs. closed group formats
  • Suggested reading and resources for each week's theme

File formats

  • PDF (print-ready, A4 and US Letter)
  • DOCX (fully editable for customization)
  • Participant worksheets as separate printable PDFs

Best for

  • Closed recovery support groups (8–15 participants)
  • Weekly 60–90 minute group sessions
  • New and experienced peer recovery specialists
  • Community health, treatment, and recovery centers

Evidence base

This resource draws on Motivational Interviewing (MI), Trauma-Informed Care principles, and Strengths-Based facilitation. Session themes were developed in collaboration with practicing peer recovery specialists and reviewed for alignment with SAMHSA guidelines.

Facilitation philosophy

Every session plan is built around the idea that structure creates safety. Clear timing, predictable flow, and low-prep facilitation give practitioners more mental bandwidth to be present with participants — rather than managing logistics in the moment.
